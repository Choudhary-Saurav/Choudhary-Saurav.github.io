A bit about me

I am Saurav, a Bioinformatics PhD candidate in Dr. Kaixiong Ye’s lab at the University of Georgia, Athens. My training spans genomics, transcriptomics, bioinformatics, and machine learning. I am actively involved in multiple projects—ranging from building a chromosome-level genome assembly and annotation for a non-model species using long-read (HiFi) sequencing, to investigating how genetic variation shapes complex traits and disease.

A major focus of my work is combining Genome-Wide Association Studies (GWAS) with single-cell RNA-seq and ATAC-seq to map genetic variants to trait-relevant cell types and uncover the regulatory programs and pathways they influence. I enjoy building clean, reproducible pipelines, analyzing large-scale datasets, and turning messy biological signals into clear, interpretable insights (the scientific version of “making sense of chaos”).

Academic Journey

My journey started at Amity University, where I completed my B.Tech in Biotechnology and built my foundation in molecular biology and genetics. I quickly realized I loved asking “why” questions, and computation felt like the fastest way to chase the answers. That curiosity took me to IIIT-Delhi for an M.Tech in Computational Biology, where I strengthened my skills in bioinformatics, data analysis, machine learning, and programming, and began working closely with large-scale biological datasets.

My master’s degree helped me bridge wet-lab knowledge with computational thinking, and it played a major role in my decision to pursue a PhD in Bioinformatics, where I now work on large-scale genomics and single-cell projects.
