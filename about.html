I'm Saurav, a Bioinformatics PhD candidate at the University of Georgia, where I explore the hidden stories written in our DNA. My work focuses on understanding how genetic variation shapes complex traits and disease using large-scale single-cell datasets.

Think of it like reading millions of tiny instruction manuals—one cell at a time—to discover what actually changes when biology goes from "healthy" to "complex".

I enjoy building clean workflows, making results easy to interpret, and turning complicated genomic data into clear biological insight. Outside research, I'm usually exploring good food, watching Hindi comedy movies, or experimenting with new tools that make analysis faster and cleaner.

If you're working on genomics, single-cell, or data-driven biology and want to collaborate—let's connect!
